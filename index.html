About Us

Ice cream is a sweetened frozen food typically eaten as a snack or dessert. It may be made from milk or cream and is flavoured with a sweetener, either sugar or an alternative, and a spice, such as cocoa or vanilla, or with fruit such as strawberries or peaches. It can also be made by whisking a flavored cream base and liquid nitrogen together. Food coloring is sometimes added, in addition to stabilizers. The mixture is cooled below the freezing point of water and stirred to incorporate air spaces and to prevent detectable ice crystals from forming. The result is a smooth, semi-solid foam that is solid at very low temperatures (below 2 °C or 35 °F). It becomes more malleable as its temperature increases.

In some countries, such as the United States, "ice cream" applies only to a specific variety, and most governments regulate the commercial use of the various terms according to the relative quantities of the main ingredients, notably the amount of cream.[1] Products that do not meet the criteria to be called ice cream are sometimes labelled "frozen dairy dessert" instead. In other countries, such as Italy and Argentina, one word is used for all variants. Analogues made from dairy alternatives, such as goat's or sheep's milk, or milk substitutes (e.g., soy, cashew, coconut, almond milk or tofu), are available for those who are lactose intolerant, allergic to dairy protein, or vegan. Ice cream may be served in dishes, for eating with a spoon, or licked from edible wafer cones. Ice cream may be served with other desserts, such as apple pie, or as an ingredient in ice cream floats, sundaes, milkshakes, ice cream cakes and even baked items, such as Baked Alaska.

Is the fear of ruining your diet plan keeping you off from a delightful bowl of ice cream? Then fret not as we have curated some homemade ice creams under 100 calories, which can make for a delicious meal replacement to fit into your diet regime. Summers are here and what can be better than indulging in cold, refreshing and sweet ice creams, and if you too have been yearning for one, then follow us through these simple recipes to satiate your love for delish ice creams that too at the comfort of home.

This healthy and tasty ice cream is worth trying. Prepare a simple green tea using two to three tea bags. Pour this mixture in a baking tray and once it attains a room temperature, add in 1 cup mango puree, mint leaves, cardamom powder, mix it all together and pour it into popsicle molds and refrigerate for 7 hours and enjoy. This ice cream has around 97 calories overall, which makes it a perfect fix for cheat days.

To make this quick ice cream, take 2 cups of almond milk, boil over medium flame till it starts reducing. Lower the flame and 2 tablespoons add cocoa powder and 1 teaspoon vanilla essence, 1 tablespoon of coffee. Allow it to cool down and add stevia/honey, whisk it well. Pour it into the setting tray and freeze for 5 hours, take it out, mix it again and again and freeze for 3-4 hours by adding some roasted almonds.

Ice creams are not always creamy, if you are a fan of popsicles, then this sweet ice cream will surely win your heart. Take a large bowl or jar of water, pour ¼ cup of lemon juice, ¼ cup ginger juice, 2 tablespoons of honey, mix it all well. Pour it in a baking tray and freeze for 3 hours, take it out and add in lemon rind, add apple bits to make it more delicious. Then freeze it well for 3-4 hours and enjoy. With around 89 calories, this simple ice cream is not just delicious but is hydrating.
